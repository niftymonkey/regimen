# Reading across conversations is two co-equal capabilities: a verdict rollup and a leverage audit

> Status: accepted (2026-06-29)

ADR-0015 made bulk judging a dumb sweep: it loops the per-conversation judge over a `list`-filtered set and persists each verdict, and it explicitly defers the cross-conversation read ("reading persisted verdicts is free and compositional ... build it when the read need is concrete"). This ADR records the direction that read should take once it is built. The read is not one feature; it is two co-equal capabilities over the same persisted verdicts, neither outranking the other. The first is a verdict rollup that aggregates the judge's per-conversation assessment prose into how work is going, the recurring patterns, and concrete remedies (its detailed design is in plans/verdict-rollup-design.md). The second is a leverage audit (a practice-adherence check) that verifies whether the engineer's own established practices (rules, conventions, and skills) are actually honored across conversations, and recommends, per practice, enforce, revise, convert to user-invoked, or retire (its detailed design is in plans/leverage-audit-design.md). Both earn their place the same way: they aggregate what no human reliably tallies, and they correct the drift between what the engineer remembers or feels and what the captured conversations actually show. A loud problem that recurs across many conversations but was never connected is as worth surfacing as a silent one no one ever noticed; the volume is not the signal, the connection is. This ADR fixes that direction and the constraints the read must satisfy. It does not build either capability or settle their wire shapes, which are designed when each is built against real persisted verdicts.

## Why two capabilities, not one report

The rollup and the leverage audit answer different questions and emit different shapes, so collapsing them hides one behind the other. The rollup answers "how is it going, what keeps happening, and what should I do about it," and its output is a narrative of patterns and remedies. The leverage audit answers "are the practices I deliberately set up being kept," and its output is a per-practice verdict with a recommendation. The practice question is the one that closes Regimen's loop: a practice is a lever in the ADR-0013 sense, and a lever that is silently unused is dead weight, which only a check that counts adherence across conversations can catch. The reason the audit is necessary and distinct is the unpredictability of model-invoked behavior: a practice expressed as a model-invoked skill may simply not fire even when appropriate, and because the work still completes and the conversation scores as accomplished, no per-conversation verdict surfaces that silent non-firing. Folding this into a general "how are they doing" report would bury the question as a footnote on the rollup. The two are co-equal because the perception-correction value applies equally to both: the silent-gap case (a practice quietly ignored) and the loud-aggregated case (a problem seen many times but never connected) are both things a human does not reliably tally, and surfacing either is the point. The model-invoked versus user-invoked distinction, leading words, and the no-op deletion test underpinning the audit's remedies are drawn from Matt Pocock's "The Missing Manual: How to Write Great Skills" (see plans/leverage-audit-design.md for the full treatment and attribution).

## A leverage audit is time-scoped

A practice has a history: it was introduced at some point, it may have been revised, it may have been retired. Judging a conversation against the current version of a practice would fault conversations that predate that practice or its current wording, manufacturing false non-adherence. So the audit assesses each conversation only against the version of the practice that existed at that conversation's time. This requires temporal provenance for practices: when each came into force, and when it changed. The recommendation the audit emits (enforce, revise, convert to user-invoked, or retire) is itself perception-correction: a practice ignored across recent conversations is a candidate to enforce, revise, convert, or retire, and the data, not the engineer's sense of it, is what makes that call.

## The judge may take the engineer's setup as expected behavior

Both capabilities want the judge to know the engineer's setup: their established practices, rules, and conventions, and the environment they work in (for example the cloud or provider they target), so a conversation is assessed against what was actually expected of it rather than against a generic baseline. Whether that setup is baked into the judge (compiled into the prompt and rubric) or queried at judge time (read from a live source of practices) is an open question, deferred to the build. Two constraints bind it either way. It must stay harness- and model-agnostic: no setup detail may be Claude-specific or model-specific, and any harness-shaped input normalizes at the capture and adapter edge per the project constraint. And it must compose with the time-scoping above: the setup the judge reasons against is the setup as of the conversation, not as of now.

## What this does not decide

It does not fix the wire shapes or storage of either capability; those are designed when each is built (and seeded in plans/verdict-rollup-design.md and plans/leverage-audit-design.md). It does not decide whether the rollup is a command, a skill, or both; the answering surface stays compositional per ADR-0013 and ADR-0015 until a concrete read need fixes it. It does not decide the per-conversation judge enrichments this read would benefit from, which are adjacent future work in the per-conversation judge and ADR-0008's rubric, not in this read direction: distinguishing "the AI fell short" from "the session was never engaged" so a non-accomplished verdict carries real signal (the Outcome ordinal today is a coarse four-value rank, abandoned < partial < accomplished-with-correction < accomplished-cleanly, two of whose four values are non-accomplished and currently indistinguishable as to cause); richer per-label criteria; and a friction or effort axis distinct from accomplishment. These sharpen the verdicts the read aggregates, but they live in the per-conversation judge, not here.

## Considered options

- Fold the read into one "how are they doing" rollup. Rejected: it buries the leverage-audit question, which is the one that closes Regimen's see-act-validate loop by catching a lever that is silently unused. The two answer different questions and emit different shapes, and co-equal billing keeps the leverage audit from being a footnote on the rollup.
- Build the read now, alongside the sweep. Rejected, consistent with ADR-0015: the persisted verdicts are free to read, the read need is not yet concrete, and fixing a wire shape before there is a real query designs the surface cold.
- Judge practice-adherence against the current version of each practice. Rejected: it faults conversations that predate a practice or its current wording, manufacturing false non-adherence. The audit is time-scoped to the practice version in force at each conversation's time.
- Bake the engineer's setup into the judge as the only option, or query it as the only option. Deferred, not decided: both are viable, the choice is made at build time, and either way the setup must stay harness- and model-agnostic and compose with time-scoping.
- Put the per-conversation judge enrichments (engaged-versus-fell-short, per-label criteria, friction axis) in this ADR. Rejected as out of scope: they belong to the per-conversation judge and rubric (ADR-0008), not the cross-conversation read. They are noted here only as adjacent future work, because the read's signal quality depends on them.
